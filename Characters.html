Saiyans

Saiyans are a proud and battle-hardened race originating from Planet Vegeta in Dragon Ball Z. They are known for their unparalleled combat instincts, aggressive nature, and relentless desire to grow stronger. Unlike many species, Saiyans thrive in battle, constantly seeking powerful opponents to challenge themselves. Saiyans can analyze opponents mid-battle and adjust their tactics accordingly. Even without formal training, they instinctively know how to fight. Through sheer determination and training, they constantly break past their natural limits. Saiyans learn to use Ki (energy attacks) faster than most races, leading to powerful techniques like the Kamehameha and Final Flash. Everything about Saiyans—from their natural strength to their love for fighting and constant evolution—makes them one of the most powerful warrior races in Dragon Ball Z.

Saiyans Throughout The Series:

Goku

Goku is the main protagonist of the Dragon Ball franchise, created by Akira Toriyama. He's a Saiyan warrior who was sent to Earth as a baby. Originally named Kakarot, Goku was raised by Grandpa Gohan and grew up with a pure heart and a love for fighting strong opponents. As a child (Dragon Ball), Goku trains in martial arts, makes friends, and goes on adventures to find the Dragon Balls. As an adult (Dragon Ball Z), he learns about his alien heritage, defends Earth from powerful enemies like Frieza, Cell, and Majin Buu, and becomes the first to achieve forms like Super Saiyan. In Dragon Ball Super, Goku reaches godlike power levels, including Super Saiyan God, Super Saiyan Blue, and Ultra Instinct, battling threats from alternate universes and gods. He's also appeared in Dragon Ball GT (non-canon) and many movies, often saving the universe with his strength, kindness, and never-give-up spirit.

Vegeta

Vegeta is one of the most iconic characters in the Dragon Ball franchise. He's the Prince of the Saiyan race, originally introduced as a ruthless villain, but over time, he becomes one of Earth's greatest defenders and Goku's lifelong rival. Vegeta arrives on Earth as a merciless Saiyan warrior, seeking the Dragon Balls. He fights Goku in a legendary battle but is eventually defeated and retreats. Though he starts off cold and prideful, Vegeta slowly begins to care for the people around him—especially Bulma (whom he marries) and their children (Trunks and Bulla). He never fully loses his pride but evolves into a true hero. This is one of the core dynamics of the series. Vegeta is obsessed with surpassing Goku, constantly training to become stronger. Unlike Goku, who fights for fun, Vegeta fights to prove his strength and protect his family. Namek/Frieza Saga: Becomes an anti-hero, briefly teams up with the Z Fighters. Cell Saga: Achieves Super Saiyan but is outclassed by others. His arrogance nearly causes disaster. Majin Buu Saga: Lets himself be possessed to gain power (Majin Vegeta), then sacrifices himself to save Earth—a major turning point for his character. Dragon Ball Super: Reaches god-level forms like Super Saiyan Blue, trains with Whis, and even surpasses Goku in moments. Notably, he defeats Toppo (a God of Destruction candidate) and shows massive growth emotionally and morally. At his core, Vegeta is a proud warrior who evolves from a cold, vengeful prince into a powerful protector, husband, and father—while never losing his fierce edge or pride in his Saiyan heritage.

Broly

Broly is a Saiyan warrior known for his immense power and rage-fueled fighting style. Originally introduced in the non-canon 1993 film Dragon Ball Z: Broly - The Legendary Super Saiyan, he was portrayed as a destructive, unstable villain with a deep hatred for Goku due to trauma from their time as infants. In 2018, Broly was reimagined and officially made canon in Dragon Ball Super: Broly. This version of Broly has a tragic backstory—exiled as a baby by King Vegeta due to his overwhelming power. He grows up on a harsh planet with his father Paragus, who trains and controls him. After being discovered by Frieza, Broly is brought to Earth and ends up battling Goku and Vegeta. Unlike his original version, this Broly is more sympathetic and misunderstood, acting out of confusion and manipulation rather than malice. His raw strength allows him to push past limits, eventually forcing Goku and Vegeta to fuse into Gogeta to stop him. Goku later befriends Broly, showing him kindness and offering to help him control his power, hinting at Broly's future as more of an ally than a villain.

Trunks

Trunks is the son of Vegeta and Bulma, making him a hybrid Saiyan-Earthling with immense potential. He has two major versions in the series: the future Trunks and the present Trunks. Future Trunks comes from a dystopian timeline where Goku dies from a heart virus and the Androids wreak havoc. He travels back in time using a time machine built by his mother to warn the Z Fighters and help prevent his future. This version is serious, noble, and battle-hardened, becoming a fan-favorite for his dramatic entrance and defeat of Frieza. Present Trunks, on the other hand, is born during the main timeline and grows up alongside Goten, becoming a more playful and mischievous character. The two fuse to become Gotenks, a powerful yet cocky fusion warrior. Trunks also plays a central role in Dragon Ball Super, especially in the Goku Black arc, where he returns to seek help against a powerful foe threatening his future once again. Known for his signature purple hair and sword, Trunks is a blend of his parents' strengths—Bulma's intelligence and Vegeta's pride and fighting spirit.

Gohan

Gohan is the first son of Goku and Chi-Chi, and one of the most important characters in the Dragon Ball series. Unlike his father, Gohan is gentle, intelligent, and more academically focused, though he possesses incredible hidden power due to his hybrid Saiyan-human heritage. As a child, he's trained by Piccolo, who becomes a mentor and father figure to him. Gohan plays a critical role in major battles, especially during the Cell Saga, where he becomes the first to achieve Super Saiyan 2 and defeats Cell in a climactic showdown, showcasing his true potential. Despite being one of the strongest fighters, Gohan chooses to focus on his studies and family life rather than constant training. In Dragon Ball Super, he rediscovers his fighting spirit during the Tournament of Power and reaches a new level of strength, known as Gohan Beast in the movie Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero, where he once again takes the spotlight. Gohan represents the balance between brains and brawn and often serves as a symbol of untapped potential and inner strength.

Goten

Goten is the second son of Goku and Chi-Chi, and the younger brother of Gohan. He closely resembles his father in appearance and personality, showing a cheerful and carefree nature. Despite his young age, Goten demonstrates incredible strength and becomes a Super Saiyan with ease, something that took older Saiyans years to achieve. He quickly forms a close friendship with Trunks, and the two are often seen training, playing, or getting into mischief together. Goten and Trunks learn the Fusion Dance and become Gotenks, a powerful but cocky warrior who plays a key role during the Majin Buu saga. Though Goten has immense potential, he's not as battle-driven as Goku or Vegeta and doesn't play a central role in later arcs like Dragon Ball Super, where he mostly appears during peaceful times. His character represents the next generation of fighters and the lighthearted spirit of childhood in the Dragon Ball universe.

Pan

Pan is the daughter of Gohan and Videl, and the granddaughter of Goku and Mr. Satan. She's a quarter-Saiyan with a bold personality and a strong sense of adventure. Even as a toddler, Pan shows remarkable strength and energy, capable of flying and performing basic martial arts techniques without formal training. In Dragon Ball Super, she's mostly seen as a baby, but her potential is hinted at multiple times. In Dragon Ball GT, which is non-canon, Pan is a major character—older and very independent, traveling the galaxy with Goku and Trunks on various adventures. In Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero, she starts training under Piccolo, following in her father's footsteps, and begins to show serious promise as a fighter. Despite her young age, Pan is brave, confident, and often eager to prove herself, embodying the Saiyan spirit passed down through her family.

Earthlings

Earthlings in Dragon Ball Z refer to the humans and human-like beings who inhabit Planet Earth. While they lack the overwhelming power of Saiyans or other alien races, some Earthlings possess exceptional fighting skills, intelligence, and unique abilities that allow them to compete in battles far beyond normal human limits.

Earthlings Throughout The Series:

Krillin

Krillin is one of the longest-running and most beloved characters in the Dragon Ball series. He starts off as a young monk and becomes Goku's best friend and training partner under Master Roshi. Though he's human and doesn't have the raw power of Saiyans, Krillin proves himself through determination, courage, and skill. He plays important roles throughout the series, especially during the Saiyan and Namek sagas, and is notably killed by Frieza, which triggers Goku's transformation into a Super Saiyan. Krillin is known for his clever fighting style, signature techniques like the Destructo Disc, and his strong sense of loyalty. Over time, he steps away from intense battle to live a peaceful life, marrying Android 18 and having a daughter named Marron. Despite his retirement from serious combat, he returns to help during critical moments like the Tournament of Power in Dragon Ball Super. Krillin represents the heart of the series—brave, kind, and always there for his friends no matter the odds.

Tien

Tien is a disciplined and serious martial artist who first appears as a rival to Goku during the original Dragon Ball series. He starts off as an antagonist trained under the Crane School but eventually reforms and becomes a loyal ally to the Z Fighters. Tien is known for his intense training ethic, stoic personality, and powerful techniques like the Tri-Beam (Kikoho), Multi-Form, and Solar Flare. Despite being human (or near-human with some unique traits like his third eye), he consistently pushes his limits to stand alongside much stronger foes. Throughout Dragon Ball Z, he participates in battles against the Saiyans, Cell, and Majin Buu, often stepping in to buy time or assist his friends, even when he knows he's outmatched. In Dragon Ball Super, he returns for key events like the Tournament of Power, proving his enduring dedication to martial arts and protecting Earth. Tien is respected for his honor, independence, and unwavering determination.

Yamcha

Yamcha is one of the original characters introduced in the Dragon Ball series, first appearing as a desert bandit who tries to rob Goku and Bulma but eventually becomes their ally. He's a skilled martial artist trained under Master Roshi and is known for his signature move, the Wolf Fang Fist. In the early days of Dragon Ball, Yamcha is a strong and confident fighter, even entering the World Martial Arts Tournaments. However, as the series progresses and stronger characters like the Saiyans are introduced, Yamcha becomes outclassed in power. Despite this, he remains a loyal friend and member of the Z Fighters, often providing comic relief. One of his most famous moments is being defeated by a Saibaman during the Saiyan Saga, which became a long-running meme in the fandom. Outside of combat, Yamcha is laid-back and good-natured, with a history of dating Bulma before she ends up with Vegeta. Though he steps back from the front lines in later series, Yamcha's legacy as an original Dragon Ball hero remains intact.

Spiritual Beings

Spiritual beings encompass gods, deities, and other supernatural entities that influence the universe's balance. These spiritual beings shape the Dragon Ball cosmos, governing life, death, destruction, and creation across multiple universes.

Spiritual Beings Throughout The Series:

Kais

The Kais are divine beings in the Dragon Ball universe responsible for overseeing and maintaining balance in the universe. They are part of a hierarchical structure of gods, with each universe having its own set. The lower-level Kais, known as Supreme Kais or Shinjin, watch over the creation and natural order of life, while their counterparts, the Gods of Destruction, handle the task of erasing and balancing excess. The most commonly seen Kais in the series include King Kai (North Kai), who trains Goku after his death and is known for his wisdom and bad jokes, and the Supreme Kai, who becomes involved during the Majin Buu saga. There are four directional Kais (North, South, East, West) and a Grand Kai who oversees them. Above them all is the Grand Supreme Kai, a high-ranking deity who once fought Majin Buu and whose essence resides within him. Kais are not as physically powerful as some fighters but play a crucial role in the cosmic order and spiritual realm of the Dragon Ball universe.

Gods

In the Dragon Ball universe, gods are powerful celestial beings who oversee various aspects of existence and maintain cosmic balance. The most prominent are the Gods of Destruction, like Beerus from Universe 7, whose role is to destroy planets or civilizations to prevent overpopulation and ensure growth. They are paired with Supreme Kais, who create and nurture life, forming a balance between creation and destruction. Above them are beings like the Grand Priest and Zeno, the Omni-King, who sits at the top of the hierarchy and can erase entire universes effortlessly. Gods are typically vastly more powerful than mortals, with unique abilities and divine auras. They often operate from otherworldly realms and abide by strict hierarchies and protocols. Some gods, like Whis and other angel attendants, serve as mentors and guides to the Gods of Destruction, while remaining neutral and highly skilled. While many gods in the series start off as distant or fearsome figures, several develop bonds with mortals, particularly Goku and his allies, leading to new alliances and power developments.

Angels

Angels in the Dragon Ball universe are divine beings who serve as attendants and mentors to the Gods of Destruction in each universe. They are incredibly powerful, often surpassing the gods they serve, and possess vast knowledge, refined martial arts skills, and the ability to manipulate time and space. Each angel is assigned to a specific universe and is responsible for guiding and training its God of Destruction while remaining completely neutral and unbiased in all matters. Whis, the angel of Universe 7, is the most well-known, serving as Beerus's attendant and also training Goku and Vegeta. Angels are the children of the Grand Priest, who himself is one of the most powerful entities in the multiverse and advisor to Zeno, the Omni-King. They are calm, polite, and maintain order through adherence to strict divine laws. If a God of Destruction dies, the angel becomes inactive until a new god is appointed. Despite their peaceful demeanor, their power is overwhelming, and their role is vital in maintaining cosmic balance.

Villains

The Dragon Ball series is known for its iconic villains, many of whom evolve over time or push the heroes to reach new levels of power. These antagonists are often threats not only to the Earth, but to the entire universe or multiverse.

Villains Throughout The Series:

Dragon Ball Villains

Emperor Pilaf: A comedic villain who tries to steal the Dragon Balls to take over the world. More annoying than dangerous. General Red & The Red Ribbon Army: A militaristic group seeking world domination. They become the foundation for later android-related storylines. King Piccolo (Piccolo Daimao): The first truly serious villain — an ancient demon who wants to plunge the world into darkness. Defeated by Goku, but later reincarnated as Piccolo (his son/clone), who becomes an ally.

Dragon Ball Z Villains

Vegeta & Nappa (Saiyan Saga): Initially ruthless Saiyans sent to conquer Earth. Vegeta becomes a fan-favorite and later joins the Z Fighters. Frieza (Frieza Saga): The tyrannical emperor of the universe, responsible for destroying Planet Vegeta (the Saiyan homeworld). His battle with Goku leads to the iconic Super Saiyan transformation. Cell (Cell Saga): A bio-engineered android made from the DNA of powerful fighters. He absorbs Androids 17 and 18 to reach his "Perfect" form and creates the Cell Games. Majin Buu (Buu Saga): A magical, chaotic being with multiple forms — from childlike and innocent to pure evil. Buu's saga features fusions, god-tier fights, and the debut of Vegito.

Dragon Ball Super Villains

Beerus: Not a villain per se, but a God of Destruction. Introduced as a threat, but becomes more of a mentor figure. Frieza (Resurrected): Returns with a new form (Golden Frieza) and a grudge. Goku Black / Zamasu (Future Trunks Saga): A dark version of Goku created by Zamasu, a corrupted Supreme Kai apprentice. Their plan: "Zero Mortals." Jiren (Tournament of Power): A stoic warrior from Universe 11. Not evil, but an obstacle for Universe 7's survival.

Dragon Ball GT Villains

Baby: A Tuffle parasite who seeks revenge on the Saiyans. He possesses Vegeta and nearly takes over Earth.
Super 17: A fusion of two Android 17s — powerful and emotionless.
Omega Shenron: Born from the negative energy of the Dragon Balls, he represents the corrupted consequence of overusing the
wish-granting orbs.
